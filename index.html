Answers to the questions applicants ask most often.

💰 Money & Costs

The N-400 filing fee is $760 (or $710 online). Fee waivers are available for low-income applicants using Form I-912.

Yes. Many non-profit organizations offer fee assistance. See the resources section below.

🔒 Privacy & Safety

USCIS keeps your application confidential. Information is protected under federal privacy laws.

Yes. VAWA (Violence Against Women Act) protections may apply. Contact a legal aid organization for confidential help.

⚖️ Criminal Background

Yes. You must disclose ALL arrests, charges, citations, and detentions — even if dismissed, expunged, or sealed.

A single DUI is typically not a bar, but two or more DUIs may be. Consult an immigration attorney.

Juvenile offenses may still need to be disclosed. Consult an attorney for guidance.

📋 Taxes

File the missing return(s) before your interview. Bring IRS tax transcripts as proof.

Yes. Bring IRS tax transcripts for the past 3–5 years (depending on your track).

✈️ Travel

Yes, but extended travel during the process could cause issues. Keep trips short.

Not necessarily. Trips under 6 months are usually fine. 6–12 months need explanation. Over 1 year may require restarting.

💳 Green Card Issues

Yes. An expired green card does not affect your immigration status. You can apply with an expired card.

If you've been an LPR for 5+ years, yes (using the standard 5-year track, not the 3-year married track).

📝 General Process

Currently 6–12 months from filing to oath ceremony, depending on your location.

A USCIS officer will review your N-400, test your English, ask civics questions, and ask about your background.

Only if you qualify for the English test exemption. Otherwise, the interview is conducted in English.
